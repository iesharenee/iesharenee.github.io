Iesha Magallanes

The Heads of Africana Womanhood

Alter installtion that reflects the beauty of Africana womanhood in Black women.
Paper, Wire, Glue, and Metal

I ultimately created an alter installation to give thanks to the ancestors that paved the way for Africana people.

I placed the paper flowers around the paper head and on the individual head wireframes to embody the beauty of africana strength held within the Black woman.

I created flowers out of green, black, and red paper to symbolize an africana essence.

For my paper heads, I did paper mache on a plastic human doll head. I had to wait about two days for it to dry. Once dried, I had to cut it and slowly peel it off without tearing it. Once off, I had to glue it back together to make the standing paper head.

I began my installation by creating three woman heads made completely out of wire. I created the head shapes by soldering wire to itsef and then I hand twisted and bent wire onto the heads to create textures that mimicked Black hair.
